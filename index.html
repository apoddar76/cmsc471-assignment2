Assignment Write-Up

White-Hat

This visualization uses the OECD dataset Air Emissions - Greenhouse Gas Emissions Inventories, sourced from National Inventory Submissions 2025 to the UNFCCC. The dataset includes annual, human-caused greenhouse gas emissions across countries, pollutants, and source categories. It reports emissions in CO₂-equivalent units and includes categories such as energy, agriculture, waste, and industrial processes.

For our design, we used two views. First, a line chart shows how emissions changed over time for the five largest emitters in the latest year, because line charts are effective for accurately showing trends. Second, a sector breakdown bar chart shows which source categories contribute most to emissions in one selected country (which the user selects from a dropdown). There is filtering present due to the drop-down, and we perform aggregation to determine country-year totals and sector-totals (which then sort the top countries). We also implemented tooltips for the second graph.

This page is designed to be easy to interpret for a general audience. It explicitly highlights the filtering at the top so it is clear what is happening on the page and the user has autonomy over what they see. The visualization also avoids distorted scales and misleading color maps, as everything is scaled in a standard, consistent way, and the colors are easy to differentiate. There are clear captions to explain what each graph shows and all the axes are labeled thoroughly. For the sector graph, the user can hover on the bars to see the exact numbers if they want more detailed information. Finally, for both the line chart and bar chart, sizes and distances between the lines/bars are kept consistent so that it is easy to make accurate comparisons between different countries.
